Good Kid /M.A.A.D City A Hooked? review

Kendrick Lamar is no stranger to critical and general public acclaim,
due to his willing explore into new sounds despite us fans being hesitant,
so Mr Lamar's not just a great rapper
he is also a great composer and curator of sounds.

I listened to the project as a whole in early 2020 a good 8 years after its release
it truly upset me that I never thought to listen earlier but after thinking about it ,it came to me,
no way a 12 year old would have related and understood lamar.

GK/MC has been manicured and picked at by Mr lamar ,like a chef with twezers perfecting
a three stared michellin dish ,for their customers.

Small details grace this album ,like the static sounds of a TV during the conversations laced throughout this project.
The album starts off with a tape being inserted into a player,
the person watching is us ,the listener looking back at his teenage years ,analysing his actions and the environment he grew up in,
I believe the reason we never hear Lamar speak with his friends in these conversations
is because he is speaking through his raps ,it could aslo reflect that he rarely talked unless he was rapping

this makes one appreciate and gush on the attention to detail Mr Lamar puts into this project.
I know they are plenty more easter eggs just waiting to be found which keeps me coming to listen to GK/MC
listening is key in the album ,listening because I know not to take anything any sounds for granted in this Project
everything feels purposeful and resulting in a multiple layered opus.
